WILD Wireframes v4 · 2026-05-21

WILD: Would I be a Living Donor?

An app to help you discover your own policy of living donation.

The seven screens, in order

Step 1

Welcome

Two-sentence promise. Trauma-informed safety cues sitting inside the design, not buried in a footer.

Step 2

The donation journey

Ten stages, end to end. Where on it the user is likely standing right now.

Step 3

The map

Exhibit-hall treatment of the five ecosystems. One illustrated motif per zone, one warm “suggested” zone.

Step 4

Inside an ecosystem

Recipient relationship. The eighteen stories typeset as a reader’s table of contents, not a CRM list.

Step 5

Scenario card

Melinda’s story as a page from a small book. One statement at a time. Calm reveal between statements.

Step 6

Summary

The two-voice document, organised by donation-process stage. Open questions reframed as an agenda for the next conversation.

Step 7

Today (returning)

What a returning user sees: their summary first, with one obvious next move on top.

Step 8

Library

The articles the glossary and the mandated-reading drawer pull from. Browsable on its own.

Step 9

A library article

Reader treatment of a single article. The drawer uses the same body.

Step 10

Notes

Everything you’ve written in your own words, in one place. Free notes and per-story notes.

What changed from v3

  • Two type families instead of a system stack. Newsreader (serif) carries the quoted voices: scenario stems, summary statements, the user’s own words. Inter (sans) carries chrome, buttons, eyebrows. The two-voice document gets half its provenance from the type contrast.
  • Colour held to two confident moments. Slate blue for nav and primary action. Terracotta in exactly two places: the map’s “suggested” zone and the Summary’s “Take into your next conversation” section. Reserving it gives it semantic weight.
  • Scenario card rebuilt as a reader page. Serif stem, one statement at a time, calm 200ms reveal, easy change-of-mind, a single quiet “What’s next?” footer instead of three competing action surfaces.
  • Map rebuilt as an exhibit hall. The five zones get hand-drawn-feeling SVG motifs and confident typesetting. The suggested zone spans the full row instead of being a tile with a badge.
  • Summary rebuilt from the session-arc spec. Two-voice provenance, organised primarily by donation-process stage, with a stance-axis toggle one tap away. Unresolved items reframed as a warm agenda for the next conversation rather than residual uncertainty.
  • “Today” surface added for returning users. Lands them on their summary with one obvious next move at the top: pick up where they left off, or try an ecosystem they haven’t visited.
  • Trauma-informed-design checklist adopted. No streaks, no points, no countdown timers, no surprise modals. An always-visible “Leave for now” affordance lives in the header. One toast micro-affirmation, dismissable, no sound.

What was kept from v3

  • The five-ecosystem navigation and the zoo-map metaphor.
  • The ten-stage donation-process orientation.
  • The Agree / Disagree / Not sure yet response trio, with the third given equal visual weight.
  • The fictional-composite disclaimer, the no-account model, the “nothing leaves your device” posture.
  • The information architecture broadly. v4 is a visual and interaction rebuild, not a re-architecture.

Decisions deferred to user testing

  • Self-hosted versus CDN fonts (CDN here for prototype convenience; self-host for production privacy).
  • Whether the warm secondary stays a single hue or splits into per-zone hues across the map.
  • Whether the per-card journal pill stays alongside a tab-bar Notes destination, or whether one of the two is redundant.
  • Whether an illustrated literal-cartography map belongs on the v5 backlog.

All scenarios are fictional composites. Library content is at pending_review status. Counts shown here are illustrative for the prototype; the production bank is planned to hold 100–200 scenarios at launch.
